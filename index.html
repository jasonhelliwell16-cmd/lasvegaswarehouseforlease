Frequently Asked Questions

Warehouse For Rent Las Vegas — FAQ

Common questions from tenants searching for warehouse space for rent in Las Vegas — from small warehouse units to large distribution bays.

We currently have units from 2,000 SF to 20,000 SF on the Las Vegas West Strip, ranging from small flex bays suited to a van-based operation or e-commerce fulfilment, through to large-bay warehouses with loading docks for distribution businesses. Units up to 20,000 SF are coming soon — register your interest and Jason will contact you as soon as they come online.
NNN means you pay the base rent plus your proportionate share of property taxes, building insurance, and common area maintenance (CAM). It is the industry standard for commercial and industrial leases in Las Vegas. The benefit to tenants is a lower base rate — NNN leases typically have lower face rents than gross leases because the landlord's risk is reduced. Jason will walk you through the total occupancy cost before you commit to anything.
Selected units offer 200A up to 1,200A 3-phase power. You need 3-phase if you're running CNC machines, large compressors, commercial HVAC, welding equipment, industrial ovens, or any motor over ~5HP. If you're running a TikTok shop, e-commerce fulfilment, or light assembly, standard single-phase is usually sufficient. Not sure? Tell Jason what equipment you're running and he'll advise on which unit fits.
Nevada has no state corporate income tax, no personal income tax, no franchise tax on income, and no inventory tax. Compared with California (8.84% corporate / up to 13.3% personal) or New York (6.5% corporate / up to 10.9% personal), the savings for owner-operated businesses can be substantial — often six figures annually. Las Vegas also offers lower commercial rents than Los Angeles, San Francisco, or New York, compounding the cost advantage. Many of Jason's tenants have relocated specifically for this combination of low tax and affordable industrial space.
For shell units, allow 2–6 weeks for any buildout work depending on scope. Fully built-out units (like Suite 108 and Suite 202) can be occupied within days of signing. Jason can often facilitate a fast-track showing-to-keys timeline for tenants with an urgent requirement — text him at 702-863-6001 and tell him your timeline.
Most units are offered on 12–36 month NNN leases. Longer terms are available and often attract better rates. Jason works with landlords directly and can negotiate terms that fit your business stage — whether you're just starting out or expanding an established operation. He'll be straight with you about what's achievable.
Most West Strip industrial buildings allow tenant signage on the unit fascia. Specifics depend on the building and your lease terms. If exterior signage is important to your business — particularly if you have walk-in customers — flag it when you enquire and Jason will confirm what's permitted on any unit you're considering.
Flex units in Las Vegas typically permit a showroom or customer-facing front area combined with a warehouse or production area at the rear. Pure retail is generally not permitted in industrial-zoned buildings. If you're running a trade showroom, a product demo space, or a contractor's office with a yard, flex is usually the right fit. Jason can confirm zoning specifics for any unit.
