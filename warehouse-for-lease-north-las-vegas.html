Market Context

Why Businesses Choose North Las Vegas Industrial Space

North Las Vegas has outpaced every other Las Vegas submarket in industrial development over the past decade — here's why.

North Las Vegas sits at the intersection of two major highways — Interstate 15 and US-95 — giving tenants direct freight access south to the Las Vegas Strip and McCarran corridor, north toward Utah, and west toward California. This connectivity is the single biggest reason national distribution and logistics companies have concentrated their Nevada operations along the Rancho, Losee, Cheyenne, and Craig Road corridor rather than other parts of the valley.

Unlike the West Strip or Henderson submarkets, North Las Vegas still has substantial undeveloped industrial land. That has allowed developers to build modern, large-format distribution facilities with 28 to 40 foot clear heights, ESFR sprinkler systems, and dozens of dock-high loading positions — specifications that simply don't exist in older industrial stock closer to the Strip. For a business that needs a true distribution-grade facility rather than a converted small-bay unit, North Las Vegas is usually the only realistic option in the valley.

North Las Vegas industrial space also benefits from comparatively lower rents than the West Strip or Airport submarkets, while still offering strong access to the broader Las Vegas labor market. For manufacturing and assembly operations this combination — lower occupancy cost plus a skilled, available workforce — has made the corridor increasingly attractive to businesses relocating from California, where both industrial rents and labor costs run significantly higher.

The submarket has also become a magnet for e-commerce and last-mile fulfilment operators. With population growth across the Las Vegas valley continuing to accelerate, North Las Vegas distribution facilities are positioned to serve a rapidly growing consumer base without the congestion and cost premium of warehouse space closer to the Strip.

The Corridor

North Las Vegas Industrial Corridors

Four key roads define the North Las Vegas industrial submarket — each with its own character and tenant profile.

🛣️

Rancho Drive

Central spine of the North Las Vegas industrial area with direct access to US-95 and I-15. Mix of small-bay flex and mid-size distribution buildings serving local and regional businesses.

📦

Losee Road

Home to some of the newest large-format distribution buildings in the valley. Modern construction, high clear heights, and strong dock-door ratios attract regional and national logistics operators.

🚛

Cheyenne Avenue

Major east-west corridor connecting to I-15. Home to large 3PL and e-commerce distribution operations including national carriers. Excellent for bulk warehouse and cross-dock facilities.

🏭

Craig Road

Established industrial park area with a mix of older bulk warehouse stock and newer infill development. Often the best value per square foot in the North Las Vegas submarket.

The Process

How to Lease Industrial Space in North Las Vegas

Leasing warehouse space in North Las Vegas follows a clear process — here's what to expect.

Start with your specific requirements. North Las Vegas inventory ranges enormously in size and specification — from a 2,000 SF flex unit on Rancho Drive to a 100,000 SF cross-dock facility on Craig Road. Before searching, define your square footage range, required clear height, number of loading doors, power requirements, and target move-in date. The more specific you are, the faster Jason can identify the right match — including off-market options not listed publicly.

Tour the corridor, not just one building. Because North Las Vegas spans four distinct sub-corridors — Rancho Drive, Losee Road, Cheyenne Avenue, and Craig Road — it's worth touring more than one location before deciding. Each corridor has a different mix of building ages, specifications, and price points. A short drive between Losee Road and Craig Road can reveal meaningfully different value depending on what you need.

Compare total occupancy cost. North Las Vegas leases are typically structured as NNN, meaning your total monthly cost is base rent plus a share of property taxes, insurance, and common area maintenance. When comparing two North Las Vegas warehouse options, always compare full NNN cost rather than headline base rent — a lower base rent with high CAM charges can end up costing more than a higher base rent with efficient operating expenses.

Negotiate from a position of market knowledge. North Las Vegas landlords — particularly those with larger distribution buildings — are accustomed to negotiating on free rent periods, tenant improvement allowances, and renewal options for qualified tenants. Having a broker who knows current North Las Vegas asking rents and recent comparable deals gives you real leverage in these conversations, especially on longer-term leases.
