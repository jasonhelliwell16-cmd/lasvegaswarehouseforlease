Market Context

Why Businesses Choose Henderson Industrial Space

Henderson has built one of the most diversified industrial bases in the Las Vegas Valley — here's why.

Henderson sits roughly 16 miles southeast of downtown Las Vegas with direct access to Interstate 515, Interstate 215, US-95, and State Route 146 (St. Rose Parkway). That highway network connects Henderson industrial tenants efficiently to the rest of the Las Vegas Valley, to Harry Reid International Airport, and to interstate freight routes — without the congestion that comes with West Strip or downtown locations.

Unlike submarkets defined by a single corridor, Henderson industrial space is spread across several distinct areas — Midway, Gibson Springs, West Henderson, St. Rose, Whitney Ranch, and the newer Black Mountain area — each with its own mix of building vintages. Midway carries the largest concentration of listings in the city, while newer development near Black Mountain is bringing Class A specifications, including 28-foot clear heights and modern fire suppression systems, that don't exist in Henderson's older industrial stock.

Henderson also benefits from Foreign Trade Zone 252 status, which can offer real tax and duty advantages for tenants involved in international trade, and from Union Pacific Railroad access for businesses with heavy or large freight needs. Combined with a diversified local economy that extends well beyond the casino and hospitality industry that defines much of the Las Vegas Strip, Henderson has attracted a genuinely broad mix of industrial users.

Henderson has also seen meaningful new industrial construction in recent years, including industrial condo developments that let qualified tenants build ownership equity instead of leasing — a structure that's relatively uncommon elsewhere in the valley and worth considering for businesses planning a long-term stay.

The Process

How to Lease Industrial Space in Henderson

Leasing warehouse space in Henderson follows a clear process — here's what to expect.

Start with your specific requirements. Henderson inventory spans a wide range of building vintages and specifications — from a 1,500 SF flex unit built in the 1980s to a brand-new 145,000 SF industrial condo development. Before searching, define your square footage range, required clear height, number of loading doors, power requirements, and target move-in date. Building class matters more in Henderson than in some other submarkets, since rates vary widely between Class A, B, and C space.

Tour more than one submarket. Because Henderson spans several distinct areas — Midway, Gibson Springs, West Henderson, St. Rose, and the newer Black Mountain development — it's worth comparing locations before deciding. An older Gibson Springs building and a new Black Mountain industrial condo can sit a few miles apart and serve very different tenant needs at very different price points.

Compare total occupancy cost, not headline rent. Henderson leases are typically structured as NNN, meaning your total monthly cost is base rent plus a share of property taxes, insurance, and common area maintenance. A lower headline rate on an older building can end up costing more once operating expenses are factored in than a newer, more efficient building with a higher base rent.

Consider ownership, not just leasing. Henderson is one of the few Las Vegas submarkets where industrial condo ownership is genuinely available, at developments like Black Mountain Commerce Center. For businesses planning a long-term stay, comparing the economics of ownership versus a long-term lease is worth doing before committing either way.
