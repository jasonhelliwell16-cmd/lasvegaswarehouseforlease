What is NNN?

Triple net means you pay base rent plus property taxes, building insurance, and CAM. It's the standard for Las Vegas industrial leases. The upside — base rents are lower than gross leases, and you have transparency into operating costs.

Tenant Improvements

Landlords often offer a TI (tenant improvement) allowance — a contribution toward fit-out costs — in exchange for a longer lease. On a 3-year lease you might negotiate $5–15/SF in TI, which covers basic office buildout, electrical upgrades, or painting.

Security Deposits

Typically 1–3 months of base rent. Newer businesses may need 3–6 months upfront. A strong tenant rep broker can negotiate reduced deposits for established businesses with good financials and personal guarantees.

Section 7

Types of Industrial Space in Las Vegas

Not all warehouse space is the same. Understanding the different types of industrial space helps you find the right fit for your business operation.

🏭

Warehouse

Pure storage and distribution space. High bay, minimal office. Loading docks or grade-level doors. Used for bulk storage, regional distribution, and inventory management.

⚡

Flex Space

Office and warehouse combined in one unit. Front office suite attached to a warehouse bay. The most versatile commercial space type — suited to dozens of different business types.

📦

Distribution Centre

Purpose-built for high-volume goods movement. Multiple dock-high doors, large truck courts, cross-dock capability, ESFR sprinklers. Used by 3PL, e-commerce, and regional distributors.

🔧

Manufacturing

Heavy power (3-phase), reinforced floors, high clear height, and industrial zoning. Built for production, fabrication, and assembly operations with significant equipment loads.

🏢

Office Warehouse

Significant front office buildout (30–50% of total space) with attached warehouse. Ideal for businesses needing professional client space alongside storage or light production.

🪟

Showroom Warehouse

Street-facing showroom or retail area combined with a rear warehouse. Used by furniture, equipment, auto, and building material businesses that need to display product and store inventory.

🚜

Contractor Warehouse

Drive-through access, equipment storage, material staging, and a small office. Used by HVAC, electrical, plumbing, landscaping, and general contractors operating vehicle fleets.

🚛

Logistics Facility

Large-format, dock-heavy facilities optimised for freight movement. Trailer parking, secured yards, 24/7 access, and high-speed loading capability. Used by carriers, freight brokers, and 3PL operators.

How to Lease Warehouse Space in Las Vegas

The warehouse leasing process in Las Vegas follows a clear sequence. Here is what to expect from initial search to moving in.

1

Define Your Requirements

Start by documenting your must-haves: size range, location preference, power requirements, loading type, office needs, budget, and move-in timeline. The more specific you are, the faster a broker can find the right match. Use the requirements form at lasvegaswarehouseforlease.com to submit your criteria in 60 seconds.

2

Engage a Tenant Rep Broker

A tenant representation broker works exclusively for you at no cost. Their fee is paid by the landlord. They have access to on-market and off-market space, know current pricing, and can identify opportunities before they appear publicly. In a tight market like Las Vegas this access is invaluable.

3

Tour Available Properties

Your broker arranges showings of shortlisted units. Walk every space with your specific requirements in mind — check clear height, power supply, loading access, parking, and office layout. Take photos and notes. Same-day showings are common in Las Vegas for motivated tenants.

4

Compare Options

Evaluate each option against your criteria. Consider total occupancy cost — base rent plus NNN — not just the headline rate. Factor in fit-out costs, timeline, lease term flexibility, and the landlord's reputation. Your broker will prepare a comparison to help you make an objective decision.

5

Negotiate Lease Terms

Once you identify the right space your broker negotiates on your behalf — rate, NNN structure, tenant improvement allowance, free rent period, expansion options, and exit clauses. Negotiation is normal and expected. Never accept first offer terms without discussion.

6

Review Lease Documents

A commercial lease is a complex legal document. Review carefully — particularly the NNN provisions, operating expense caps, permitted use clause, subletting rights, and renewal options. Have a commercial attorney review before signing for leases over 3 years or significant square footage.

7

Complete Due Diligence

Verify zoning permits your intended use. Check power supply capacity meets your requirements. Confirm parking allocation. Review any existing violations on the property. Your broker can guide you through the due diligence checklist specific to your business type.

8

Move Into Your New Facility

Once signed and deposit paid, coordinate your move-in date with the landlord. If tenant improvement work is required allow 2-6 weeks for construction. Most move-in ready units in Las Vegas can be occupied within days of signing. Jason can often facilitate a fast-track keys-in-hand timeline for urgent requirements.
